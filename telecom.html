Telecommunications Law

Telecommunications law is the area of law that focuses on radio, satellite, cable TV, boadcasting, and broadband internet. It incorporates legal topics like administrative law, antitrust, to local government regulation, and property law.





The Spectrum

The electromagnetic spectrum is owned and controlled by the federal government, specifically the FCC. This means that it is illegal to create certain frequencies if they interfere with the rightful owner's broadcast.

Cable

The FCC also regulates cable. From forcing cable companies to carry existing broadcast channels to ensuring that they leave cable space for third parties, the FCC is entrenched in how people deliver cable services to consumers.

Telephony

Telephony regulation from the Bell breakup to consumer protection. The FCC touches on topics like federalism, economics, and the complete undoing of their system.

The Internet

Arguably the most important aspect of the FCC's job is regulating the internet. They explore fascinating topics like net neutrality to first amendment questions.
